Welcome! I am a freshman at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, pursuing an undergraduate degree in Applied Mathematics on the Pre-Medical track.

Before moving to Chapel Hill, I resided in Charlotte, North Carolina, where I volunteered at the Novant Health Presbyterian Center. During my tenure volunteering in the emergency room, I made valuable connections with the patients and their families. I got to see the tangible impact that I had on the patients, whether through their facial expressions or mentions of gratitude, which gave me a sense of purpose. Additionally, I learned to deal with a variety of situations, ranging from calm and collected patients to anxious and overwhelmed ones. I helped direct patients where they needed to be, provided them with necessities such as cutlery and toiletries, and made sure the accommodation was sanitary, teaching me the basics of patient care and what environment and attitude is necessary in order for a patient to feel welcome and well-tended to.

After gaining a liking towards medicine and the healthcare field, I later interned at Atrium Health Hospital through the Mayor's Youth Employment Program of Charlotte. As an Intern, I participated in multiple professional development sessions where several different medical professionals spoke about their careers and journeys, further familiarizing me with the lifestyle of someone in a field as demanding as medicine. Furthermore, my peers and I developed a Capstone project in accordance with the Charlotte 2040 plan–a long-term community-drive plan to accommodate for the city’s rapid growth–which addressed disparities faced by pregnant women in the Charlotte Metro Area through an app idea that helped with transportation services. Lastly, we had an industry day where I had the opportunity to use advanced surgery training technology such as robotic simulators, ones that actual training surgeons use while developing their skillset. This allowed me to further develop my interests in the field of medicine, specifically in Cardiac surgery.

Outside of the professional bubble, I love to play sports, including but not limited to basketball, volleyball, and swimming. I also enjoy watching basketball, specifically the NBA. Furthermore, I adore nature, and relish spending time outside. Guitar and logic puzzles also peak my interest, and are a favorite hobby of mine.
